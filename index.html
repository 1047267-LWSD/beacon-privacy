Privacy Policy for Beacon: Scam Protection

Last updated: July 7, 2026

Beacon: Scam Protection ("the app") is a scam-message detection tool. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it's used, and your choices.

What We Collect

Message text you submit for analysis. When you paste or type a message into Beacon's detector, that text is sent to our backend server to generate a scam/spam prediction. This message text, along with the prediction and which model produced it, is stored in our database. It is not linked to your name, email, or any other personal identifier.

Messages you flag as incorrectly predicted. If you indicate that a prediction was wrong, we store that message text, the original prediction, the correct label you provided, and which model made the prediction — so we can use it to improve future versions of our detection models. This is also not linked to any personal identifier.

Messages you choose to report to the community forum. If you submit a detected message to the public forum, that message text, its predicted category, and confidence score are stored and displayed publicly to other users of the app, along with anonymous upvote/downvote counts.

Anonymous device identifiers. To support voting and flagging on the community forum, the app uses Firebase's anonymous authentication, which assigns your device a random identifier. This identifier is not linked to your name, email, or any other personally identifying information, and cannot be used to determine who you are.

Flag reports. If you flag a forum post as inappropriate, the reason you provide is stored so we can review and remove content that violates our community standards.

What We Don't Collect

Beacon does not collect your location, contacts, photos, health data, financial information, or browsing history. We do not use advertising identifiers, and we do not sell or share your data with advertisers or data brokers.

How We Use What We Collect

  • Message text you submit is used to generate a scam/spam prediction and is retained to help evaluate and improve our detection models over time.
  • Messages flagged as incorrect predictions are retained specifically to improve model accuracy in future updates.
  • Forum posts are displayed publicly to help other users recognize similar scams.
  • Flag reports are reviewed by the app's developer to decide whether to remove reported content or take other action.

Data Retention

Message text submitted for detection, and messages flagged as incorrect predictions, are retained indefinitely to support ongoing model evaluation and improvement, unless you contact us to request deletion (see Contact, below). Reported forum posts remain visible until removed following a flag review.

Third-Party Services

Beacon uses Firebase (a Google service) for anonymous authentication and to store forum data. Firebase's own privacy practices apply to data it processes on our behalf — see Google's Privacy Policy.

Children's Privacy

Beacon is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this same URL with a revised "Last updated" date.
